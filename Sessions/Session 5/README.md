## 📖 Session Overview

Welcome to the **Making Slides** session. In both academic and professional environments, your ability to visually communicate ideas is just as important as the ideas themselves. 

This session focuses on the complete lifecycle of a presentation—from the initial planning phase to the final visual polish. We explore the core principles of slide design, common pitfalls to avoid, and the advanced tools you can use to create cohesive, engaging, and highly professional presentations.

<br>

## 🚀 Learning Outcomes

### 🎯 Skills & Takeaways
- **Strategic Planning:** Learn to write your content and define your audience completely before ever touching a design tool.
- **Visual Hierarchy:** Master the rules of typography (max 2 fonts) and understand exactly what font sizes to use for titles (36-54), subtitles (28-36), and body text (20-28).
- **Design Consistency:** Develop a consistent layout where margins, headings, and logos remain in the same position across every frame to convey a sense of completeness.
- **Audience Engagement:** Shift away from "reading slides" or turning your back to the audience by keeping text minimal and avoiding long, report-style paragraphs.
- **Resource Utilization:** Discover and leverage advanced tools for templates (Slidesgo, Slides Carnival), icons (Flaticon), and imagery (Pexels, unDraw).

<br>

## ✨ Session Highlights

> ### 💡 Important Insight
> *A slide is a visual aid, not a teleprompter. Never read directly from your slides or present long paragraphs as if you are giving a written report.*

> ### ⚡ Key Principle
> **The Font Rule:** Using too many fonts is confusing to the reader in typography. Stick to a maximum of **2 different fonts** per presentation. Never let your body text drop below size 18.

> ### 🔥 Golden Rule
> **One Slide, One Idea:** Avoid presenting more than one core point per slide. Overloading a slide with multiple concepts, complicated graphs, or excessive animations distracts the audience and dilutes your message.
